Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our platform

What is algo trading?

Algo trading uses automated systems to execute trades based on pre‑defined strategies, removing human emotions and enhancing efficiency.

How do I start using House of Algo's?

Simply sign up, install MT4, and connect to our strategies. Our system will handle the trades automatically.

Do I need trading experience?

No, our automated strategies are designed for both beginners and experienced traders.

What is the minimum investment required?

You can invest a minimum of $100. For higher investments, multiples of $1000 apply (e.g., $2000, $4000, etc.).

What are the expected returns and risks?

Returns are 2% to 5% monthly (after a 30% company share). The highest drawdown so far is 30% (53% in extreme backtesting conditions).

Is there a locking period?

No, you can stop copy trading anytime and withdraw your funds immediately.

What is the account opening procedure?

A link will be sent to you. By clicking it, you must fill in all the details (Aadhar Card, Driving License, Bank Statement, Email ID, Mobile Number). The account will be opened in 24 hours with support from our team through Anydesk.

What is the highest drawdown experienced?

Based on backtesting, the highest drawdown has reached 53% under extreme conditions like market crashes or trade wars.

What if there's a server problem?

Occasionally, VPS server issues may arise. However, our team is equipped to handle trades manually when needed.

Can tech issues be handled manually?

Yes, our expert team can manually manage trades if technical issues arise.

Risk Disclosure

Understanding the risks is essential for responsible trading

Trading Involves Risk

House of Algo's is not a financial advisor, investment firm, or brokerage. We do not provide any guarantees of profit or fixed returns. Trading in Forex and stock markets involves substantial risk, including the risk of losing all invested capital. Past performance does not guarantee future results.

Market Risk

Markets can be volatile and unpredictable. Prices can change rapidly due to economic events, news, or other factors beyond our control.

Technical Risk

Algorithmic systems may experience technical issues, connectivity problems, or execution delays that could affect trading performance.

Leverage Risk

Trading with leverage can significantly increase both potential profits and losses. Small market movements can have proportionally larger impacts on your capital.

Liquidity Risk

During certain market conditions, it may be difficult to execute trades at desired prices, potentially resulting in larger slippage or inability to exit positions.

Risk Management Tips
